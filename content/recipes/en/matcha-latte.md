# Matcha Latte

A Japanese tea house classic brought to the modern café. **3g of ceremonial-grade matcha whisked into 30ml of hot water**, then topped with **200ml of smooth, steamed milk**. The result is a vibrant green, earthy, and naturally sweet drink with a gentle caffeine lift.

---

## Ingredients

- **3g** matcha powder (ceremonial or high-quality culinary grade)
- **30ml** hot water — **92–96°C** (not boiling — boiling water burns matcha)
- **200ml** whole milk, steamed to smooth microfoam

---

## Instructions

1. **Sift the matcha.** If possible, sift **3g of matcha powder** through a fine mesh strainer into a small bowl or cup to prevent lumps.
2. **Whisk with hot water.** Add **30ml of hot water at 92–96°C** (never boiling) to the matcha. Whisk vigorously in a "W" or "M" motion with a matcha whisk (chasen) or a small regular whisk until fully dissolved with no lumps and a light froth on top.
3. **Steam the milk.** Steam **220ml** of whole milk to **60–65°C** (or **50–55°C for children**) with smooth, velvety microfoam.
4. **Combine.** Pour the matcha concentrate into the pre-warmed serving cup first. Then pour the steamed milk slowly over the top.
5. **Optional art.** The contrast of green matcha and white foam offers a beautiful latte art opportunity. Serve immediately.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Water temperature is critical:** Boiling water (100°C) will scorch matcha and create a bitter, astringent taste. Keep to **92–96°C**.
- **Matcha grade matters:** Ceremonial grade has a sweeter, more complex flavour. Culinary grade is more bitter and better masked with sweetener.
- For a **sweetened Matcha Latte**, add **10ml Monin Vanilla Syrup** or **5g honey** to the matcha paste before adding milk.
- For an **Iced Matcha Latte:** whisk matcha + water → pour over ice → add cold milk.
