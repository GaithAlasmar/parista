# Affogato

Italian for "drowned" — a **large scoop of vanilla ice cream** placed in a short glass, then flooded with a **hot double espresso** pulled fresh from the **Casadio Nettuno** on **Gia beans**. The heat from the espresso melts the edges of the ice cream creating a warm-cold, bitter-sweet experience that is unlike anything else on the menu.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 large scoop vanilla ice cream (~80–100 g)
- 1 double espresso shot (60 ml) — **Gia beans**, pulled hot
- Short glass (rocks glass or espresso cup + saucer)

---

## Method

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract a **double shot (60 ml)** from freshly ground **Gia beans** on the Casadio Nettuno. **Do not let it cool** — the shot must be served immediately while hot.
2. **Scoop the ice cream.** Place **one large, well-formed scoop** of vanilla ice cream into the short glass. Work quickly.
3. **Drown it.** Pour the **hot double espresso directly over the centre of the ice cream scoop** — in one confident pour, not a slow drizzle.
4. Serve **immediately** on a small plate or saucer. The ice cream will begin melting at the edges straight away.
5. No straw, no lid — serve with a small spoon.

---

## Tips

- **Speed is everything.** From espresso pull to table should be under 60 seconds. If the espresso cools, the contrast effect is lost.
- **Gia beans** are ideal here — their chocolate and nutty notes are a natural match for vanilla ice cream.
- For a **premium variant**: use **Miscela Bar** beans (milder, smoother espresso) and add a small square of dark chocolate on the saucer.
- Do not pre-scoop the ice cream — it must go from freezer to glass to customer in one motion.
- The correct order is ice cream first, espresso second — never the other way around.
