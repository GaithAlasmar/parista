# Doppio

Italian for "double" — a double-basket extraction delivering **twice the espresso** in one pull. The standard base shot used across the entire menu. Every latte, cappuccino, and mocha starts here.

---

## Ingredients

- **18g** freshly ground coffee — **Gia beans**
- Hot water via espresso machine (pre-heated group head)

---

## Instructions

1. **Dose.** Grind **18g** of **Gia beans** into a double-basket portafilter.
2. **Distribute.** Use a distribution tool to ensure a level, even bed of grounds.
3. **Tamp.** Apply firm, level pressure — 15–20kg. No tilting.
4. **Lock and pull.** Insert portafilter and start extraction immediately.
5. **Time the shot.** Target **25–30 seconds** for a yield of **36–40g** (weigh if possible; otherwise ~60ml volume).
6. **Assess.** Crema should be thick, hazel-brown and uniform.
7. Use immediately as the base for any milk drink, or serve as a straight doppio in a small glass.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Target yield is weight-based:** 18g in → 36–40g out (1:2 to 1:2.2 ratio).
- A heavier yield (50g+) = Lungo-style, more diluted. A lighter yield (30g) = Ristretto-style, more concentrated.
- **Water temperature:** 90–96°C on the Casadio Nettuno.
- For milk drinks, pull the doppio and immediately begin steaming milk — crema degrades after 20–30 seconds.
