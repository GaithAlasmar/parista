# Cold Brew

Cold Brew is made by steeping coarsely ground coffee in **cold water for an extended period (12–18 hours)** — no heat involved. The result is a naturally smooth, low-acid concentrate with rich chocolate and caramel undertones that no espresso can replicate.

---

## Ingredients

- **100g** coarsely ground coffee — **Gia beans** or **Miscela Bar**
- **1 litre** cold filtered water
- Large container with a lid (mason jar, cold brew pitcher, or hotel pan)
- Fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth for filtering

---

## Instructions

1. **Grind.** Grind **100g** of coffee on a coarse setting (similar to French Press grind — like raw sugar texture).
2. **Combine.** Add the ground coffee to your container. Pour over **1 litre of cold water**. Stir gently to ensure all grounds are saturated.
3. **Cover and steep.** Seal the container and place in the refrigerator for **18 hours** (minimum 12 hours, maximum 24 hours).
4. **Filter.** Pour the concentrate through a fine mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth (or a paper filter) into a clean container. Press gently — do not squeeze aggressively.
5. **Store.** Keep the filtered cold brew in the refrigerator. It stays fresh for up to **7 days**.
6. **Serve.** Pour over ice directly as-is (full strength), or dilute 1:1 with cold water for a lighter drink.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Coarse grind is critical:** Fine grinding creates bitter, muddy cold brew that is nearly impossible to filter. Go coarser than you think you need.
- **18 hours is the sweet spot** — 12 hours gives a lighter, brighter result; 24 hours risks over-extraction and bitterness.
- Cold Brew concentrate (undiluted) can be used as the base for **Iced Lattes, Cold Brew Tonics (over tonic water), or Nitro Cold Brew**.
- Yield after filtering is typically **750–850ml** from 1 litre of water due to absorption.
