# V60 Pour Over

The V60 is the gold standard of manual pour-over brewing — a **Hario V60 dripper with a paper filter, 15g of coffee, and a precise 250ml hot water pour** that extracts a clean, bright, and complex cup that reveals the full character of quality single-origin coffee.

---

## Ingredients

- **15g** coarsely-medium ground coffee — single origin or **Gia beans**
- **250ml** hot water — **92–96°C**
- Hario V60 dripper (size 01 or 02)
- V60 paper filter
- Gooseneck kettle (for controlled pour)
- Scale and timer

---

## Instructions

1. **Rinse the filter.** Place the paper filter in the V60 dripper over your serving vessel. Rinse with hot water to remove any papery taste and pre-warm the dripper. Discard the rinse water.
2. **Add the coffee.** Weigh **15g** of coffee, grind to a medium-coarse consistency (like coarse sand). Add to the filter. Create a small divot in the centre.
3. **Bloom pour.** Start your timer. Pour **30ml** of water at 92–96°C directly into the centre of the grounds. Pour in small circles to fully saturate. Wait **30–45 seconds** — the coffee will "bloom" (off-gassing CO2 will cause the grounds to bubble and rise).
4. **First pour.** Slowly pour water in concentric circles from the centre outward, then back in. Pour to **150ml** total by ~1:00 minute.
5. **Second pour.** Continue pouring in circles to reach **250ml** total by ~1:45 minute.
6. **Drain.** Let the water drain completely. The full brew time should be **2:30–3:00 minutes** from start to finish.
7. Serve immediately in a pre-warmed cup.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Grind size is everything:** Too fine = over-extracted, bitter. Too coarse = under-extracted, sour. Target a ~700 micron grind (medium-coarse).
- **Water temperature:** 92–94°C for lighter roasts; 94–96°C for darker roasts.
- **Consistent pour rate** is critical — use a gooseneck kettle. A regular kettle pours too fast and disrupts the coffee bed.
- The **bloom step** is non-negotiable — fresh coffee will produce a dramatic bloom. Stale coffee barely blooms (this is a freshness indicator).
- Target a total **brew time of 2:30–3:00 minutes** at your 250ml yield. Adjust grind accordingly.
