# Espresso Basics

The foundational knowledge every barista must internalise. These are not guidelines — they are **the standards** that define correct espresso at Parista.

---

## Single Espresso Specs

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| **Dose (Coffee In)** | 9g |
| **Yield (Liquid Out)** | 25–30ml |
| **Extraction Time** | 25–30 seconds |
| **Water Temperature** | 90–96°C |

---

## Double Espresso (Doppio) Specs

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| **Dose (Coffee In)** | 18g |
| **Yield (Liquid Out)** | 36–40g (by weight) |
| **Extraction Time** | 25–30 seconds |
| **Water Temperature** | 90–96°C |

> **Always weigh by output, not volume.** Use scales when possible. 18g in → 36–40g out = a 1:2 to 1:2.2 ratio.

---

## Espresso Variations at a Glance

| Drink | Coffee | Yield | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ristretto** | 9g | ~20ml | 15–20 sec |
| **Single Espresso** | 9g | 25–30ml | 25–30 sec |
| **Doppio** | 18g | 36–40g | 25–30 sec |
| **Lungo** | 9g | ~60ml | 30–40 sec |

## Milk Temperature Standards

Correct milk temperature is as important as correct espresso extraction.

| Drink Type | Milk Temperature |
|---|---|
| **Latte, Cappuccino, Flat White** | 60–65°C |
| **Standard milk drinks** | 60–65°C |
| **Children's drinks** | 50–55°C |
| **Customer request "extra hot"** | Max 70°C (never above) |

> ⚠️ **Never serve milk above 70°C.** Protein structure breaks down above 70°C, causing a flat, scalded taste and destroying the foam.

## Extraction Diagnosis

Use this table when a shot doesn't taste right:

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Sour / thin / watery** | Under-extraction | Grind finer OR increase dose |
| **Bitter / harsh / astringent** | Over-extraction | Grind coarser OR decrease dose |
| **Shot too fast (< 20 sec)** | Grind too coarse / under-dosed | Grind finer |
| **Shot too slow (> 35 sec)** | Grind too fine / over-dosed | Grind coarser |
| **Channelling (uneven extraction)** | Poor distribution / tamp | Redistribute and tamp level |
| **Pale/blonde crema** | Old coffee OR too coarse | Use fresh beans |

---

## Crema Colour Guide

| Crema Colour | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Deep amber / hazel-brown** | Perfect extraction ✅ |
| **Pale yellow / blonde** | Under-extracted — stop and re-pull ❌ |
| **Very dark / burnt** | Over-extracted — adjust grind ❌ |
| **White bubbles / no crema** | Stale beans or empty portafilter ❌ |

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Pull and serve within 10 seconds.** Crema begins degrading immediately after extraction.
- **Never leave a pulled shot sitting** — espresso continues to evolve chemically and the crema collapses within 30 seconds.
- **Daily calibration is non-negotiable.** The first shot of the day on the Casadio Nettuno should always be a calibration shot — adjust grind until specs are met before serving customers.
- **Tamping pressure:** approximately 15–20kg. Use a bathroom scale to calibrate your feel. Consistent tamp pressure is more important than exact force.
