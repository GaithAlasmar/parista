# Cortado

Spanish for "cut" — the espresso is "cut" by an equal part of warm steamed milk. The Cortado sits between a macchiato and a flat white: **30ml espresso, 30ml steamed milk**, no thick foam layer, served in a small glass.

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans**
- **30ml** steamed whole milk (light microfoam only)

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract a single shot (9g) into a small glass (ideally a cortado glass or small tumbler, ~120ml capacity).
2. **Steam the milk.** Steam **60–70ml** of whole milk in the pitcher to **60–65°C**. Create very light, silky microfoam — almost no bubbles. The milk should look glossy and pourable.
3. **Pour.** Gently pour **30ml** of steamed milk into the espresso. The milk cuts into the espresso without floating or separating.
4. Serve immediately. No art required, but a simple latte art heart can be attempted.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Milk temperature:** 60–65°C — critically important. Too cool and the milk doesn't integrate; too hot and the espresso flavour is killed.
- The cortado has **no froth layer** on top — this is intentional. If customers want foam, they want a cappuccino.
- The 1:1 ratio makes every ingredient count. Use fresh, well-calibrated espresso only.
- A great gateway drink for customers transitioning from black coffee to milk-based drinks.
