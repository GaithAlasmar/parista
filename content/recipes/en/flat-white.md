# Flat White

Originating from Australia and New Zealand, the Flat White is a small, strong milk drink with an emphasis on **microfoam quality over volume**. A double ristretto base is topped with **120ml of velvety microfoam** — no thick froth, just silk.

---

## Ingredients

- **60ml** double ristretto (18g, extracted to ~40ml) — **Gia beans**
- **120ml** whole milk, steamed to a glossy microfoam

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the double ristretto.** Extract **18g** of **Gia beans** and stop the extraction early at ~40ml. This gives a more concentrated, sweeter base than a standard doppio.
2. **Steam the milk.** Steam **180ml** of whole milk in the pitcher. Target **60–65°C**. Create very fine, velvety microfoam — the surface should look like wet paint, with no visible bubbles.
3. **Pour.** Begin the pour from high (~10cm) to incorporate, then lower the pitcher close to the surface and pour steadily. Create a simple latte art pattern (heart recommended).
4. Serve in a small ceramic cup (ideally 160–180ml capacity).

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Microfoam quality is everything** in a Flat White. Grainy or bubbly milk will ruin the texture — the drink should feel silky and uniform on the palate.
- The ristretto base makes the coffee-to-milk ratio **stronger than a latte** despite the similar size.
- **Milk temperature:** exactly 60–65°C. Use a thermometer until you can judge by touch.
- This is a great drink to use for practising latte art — the small canvas and microfoam quality make patterns easier to control.
