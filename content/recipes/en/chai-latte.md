# Chai Latte

A warming, aromatic blend of **spiced chai concentrate and steamed milk**. The chai brings cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, and clove into a silky milk base — comforting, complex, and caffeine-present (from the black tea base) without any coffee.

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** chai concentrate (Monin Chai Tea Syrup or equivalent liquid chai concentrate)
- **200ml** whole milk, steamed to smooth microfoam
- Optional: cinnamon powder on top, star anise garnish

---

## Instructions

1. **Chai base.** Pour **30ml of chai concentrate** into the bottom of a pre-warmed cup or glass.
2. **Steam the milk.** Steam **220ml** of whole milk to **60–65°C** with smooth, velvety microfoam. The milk's natural sweetness complements the chai spices.
3. **Pour.** Pour the steamed milk over the chai concentrate. The chai will bloom upward through the milk as it's poured.
4. **Stir briefly.** Give a gentle stir to ensure the concentrate is evenly distributed (especially if the concentrate is thick).
5. **Dust with cinnamon.** Add a light dusting of cinnamon powder on top of the foam for aroma and presentation.
6. Serve immediately.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Concentrate strength varies by brand** — start at 30ml and adjust up to 45ml for a spicier, more robust chai.
- For an **Iced Chai Latte:** chai concentrate → ice → cold milk. Stir and serve.
- For a **Dirty Chai Latte:** add a single espresso shot (9g) to the chai — a popular hybrid for customers who want both tea spice and coffee depth.
- **Cinnamon is the key garnish** — it enhances the chai's aromatic profile visually and in taste.
