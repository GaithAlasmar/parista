# Long Black

The Long Black is the **Australasian Americano** — but built in reverse. Hot water is poured first into the cup, then a double espresso is carefully floated on top. This method preserves the crema intact, delivering a stronger, more flavourful result than a traditional Americano.

---

## Ingredients

- **150ml** hot water (around 85–90°C — off-boil)
- **60ml** double espresso (18g) — **Gia beans**

---

## Instructions

1. **Pre-heat the cup.** Pour a small amount of hot water in and swirl, then discard.
2. **Pour the hot water.** Add **150ml** of hot water to the pre-warmed cup.
3. **Pull the doppio.** Extract a fresh double espresso shot (18g → ~60ml).
4. **Float the espresso.** Slowly pour the double shot over the hot water — pour gently down the side of the cup or over a spoon to preserve the crema layer on top.
5. Serve immediately without stirring. The crema floats intact on top.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Do NOT stir** before serving — the visual crema layer on top is the defining characteristic of a Long Black vs. an Americano.
- Water temperature at **85–90°C** (not boiling) prevents scalding the espresso and preserves aromatics.
- For customers who want a stronger drink: increase espresso to a triple (27g) or reduce water to 120ml.
- **Gia beans** produce a bolder Long Black; **Miscela Bar** beans give a smoother, less astringent result.
