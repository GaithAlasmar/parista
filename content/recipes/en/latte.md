# Latte

The most-ordered coffee drink in the world. A **double espresso topped with 180ml of silky steamed milk** and a thin layer of microfoam. The latte is smooth, approachable, and the perfect canvas for latte art.

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans** or **Miscela Bar**
- **180ml** whole milk, steamed to a smooth microfoam
- Thin layer of microfoam on top (~5–8mm)

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract **9g** of **Gia beans** into a pre-warmed latte glass or ceramic cup (240–300ml capacity).
2. **Steam the milk.** Steam **220ml** of whole milk in the pitcher to **60–65°C**. Create smooth, velvety microfoam with a wet-paint texture — minimal bubbles.
3. **Pour.** Begin pouring from high (~10cm) to blend the milk with the espresso. Lower the pitcher close to the surface and pour steadily into the centre.
4. **Finish with latte art.** A simple heart or tulip is the standard. Pour with confidence.
5. Serve immediately in a 240ml glass or ceramic cup.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Milk temperature:** 60–65°C for adults. For children or customers who ask for "not too hot," target 50–55°C.
- The latte has a **thin foam layer** (~5–8mm) — not a thick foam dome. More foam = cappuccino territory.
- **Miscela Bar** beans produce a naturally sweeter, smoother latte that customers with no sugar preference enjoy.
- For a stronger latte, suggest a double espresso base (18g) — this is the standard in most specialty cafés.
