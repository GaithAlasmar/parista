# Espresso

The soul of every coffee drink. A single, perfectly extracted shot of concentrated coffee — **9g of coffee, 25–30ml yield, 25–30 seconds**. No compromises, no shortcuts.

---

## Ingredients

- **9g** freshly ground coffee — **Gia beans**
- Hot water via espresso machine (pre-heated group head)

---

## Instructions

1. **Dose.** Grind **9g** of **Gia beans** to a fine, consistent grind.
2. **Distribute.** Level the grounds evenly in the portafilter with a distributor tool.
3. **Tamp.** Apply 15–20kg of pressure with a level tamp. No rocking.
4. **Lock and pull.** Insert the portafilter into the group head and start the extraction immediately.
5. **Time the shot.** Target **25–30 seconds** for a yield of **25–30ml**.
6. **Assess the crema.** Look for a deep amber-to-brown crema — this is the sign of a perfect extraction.
7. Serve immediately in a pre-warmed espresso cup.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Extraction window:** 25–30 seconds for a single shot at 9g. If it runs faster, grind finer. If it stalls, grind coarser.
- **Water temperature:** 90–96°C — the Casadio Nettuno is pre-set; do not adjust without calibration.
- **Freshness matters:** Pull the shot and serve within 10 seconds for the best crema and aroma.
- A sour shot = under-extracted (too fast / grind too coarse). A bitter shot = over-extracted (too slow / grind too fine).
