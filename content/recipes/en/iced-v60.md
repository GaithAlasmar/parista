# Iced V60 (Japanese Iced Coffee)

Unlike cold brew, Japanese Iced Coffee is brewed hot directly over ice. This flash-chilling process locks in the delicate, bright aromatics and acidity of specialty single-origin coffees that are lost in slow cold brewing.

---

## Ingredients

- **20 g** specialty coffee beans (single origin, light-medium roast)
- **200 ml** hot water (92-96°C)
- **130 g** ice (large, solid cubes)
- V60 dripper, paper filter, scale, gooseneck kettle

---

## Instructions

1. **Prep the filter:** Place the paper filter in the V60 and rinse with hot water. **Discard the rinse water** before adding ice to the server.
2. **Add ice to server:** Place exactly 130 g of ice into the carafe or server. Place the V60 dripper on top.
3. **Grind coffee:** Grind 20 g of coffee on a medium-fine setting (slightly finer than a hot V60 to compensate for less brew water). Add to the filter.
4. **Bloom:** Pour 40 ml of hot water over the coffee bed. Let it bloom for 45 seconds.
5. **Brew:** Slowly pour the remaining 160 ml of hot water in concentric circles. The hot coffee will drip directly onto the ice, melting it instantly.
6. **Finish:** The brew should finish around 2:00 - 2:30 minutes. Swirl the carafe to ensure all ice is melted and the drink is completely chilled. Pour into a glass with fresh ice.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Crucial altered ratio:** A standard hot V60 uses ~330 ml of hot water for 20 g of coffee. For Iced V60, the total liquid remains the same, but it is split into 200 ml of hot brewing water and 130 g of ice. Do NOT brew with 300 ml of hot water over ice, or the drink will be extremely watered down.
- **Grind finer:** Because you are using less hot water, the water flows through the coffee bed faster. Grind slightly finer than your hot recipe to maintain proper extraction.
- **Ice quality:** Use solid, clear ice cubes in the server. Crushed ice melts too fast and inconsistently.
