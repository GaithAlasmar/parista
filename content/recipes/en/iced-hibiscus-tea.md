# Iced Hibiscus Tea

A vibrant, ruby-red refresher that is naturally tart, caffeine-free, and incredibly thirst-quenching. 

---

## Ingredients

- **150 ml** brewed hibiscus tea (karkadeh), cooled
- **15 ml** simple syrup or honey (adjust to taste)
- **200 g** ice
- Lemon wheel for garnish

---

## Instructions

1. **Brew the hibiscus base:** Steep dried hibiscus flowers in hot water for 10 minutes. Strain and chill. (This base should be batched ahead of time).
2. **Sweeten:** In a serving glass or shaker, mix 150 ml of the cold hibiscus tea with 15 ml of simple syrup.
3. **Mix:** Stir well to dissolve the syrup into the tart tea.
4. **Serve:** Fill a tall glass with ice and pour the sweetened hibiscus tea over it.
5. **Garnish:** Add a fresh lemon wheel on the rim or inside the glass.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Adjusting sweetness:** Hibiscus is naturally very tart. Always ask the customer if they prefer it sweet or tangy, and adjust the simple syrup from 10 ml up to 25 ml accordingly.
- **Cold steeping:** For an even smoother flavour with less astringency, cold-steep the hibiscus flowers in water in the fridge for 12-24 hours instead of brewing hot.
- **Mocktail potential:** Mix with a splash of sparkling water or ginger ale for a fizzy hibiscus spritz.
