# Ristretto

Italian for "restricted." A shorter, more concentrated pull than a standard espresso — same dose, **half the water**, resulting in a sweeter, syrupy shot with none of the bitter tail end.

---

## Ingredients

- **9g** freshly ground coffee — **Gia beans** (grind slightly finer than espresso)
- Hot water (restricted to ~20ml yield)

---

## Instructions

1. **Dose.** Use **9g** of **Gia beans**, ground slightly finer than your standard espresso grind.
2. **Distribute and tamp** as normal — level, firm, consistent.
3. **Lock and pull.** Start extraction. **Stop at 20ml** — approximately 15–20 seconds.
4. **Watch the stream.** The extraction should darken quickly from golden to a rich amber, then stop before any pale/blonding appears.
5. Serve immediately in a pre-warmed espresso cup. The ristretto should have a thick, dark crema.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Stopping point is critical:** A ristretto stopped too late becomes a regular espresso. Stop the moment the stream begins to lighten (blonde).
- The flavour profile is noticeably **sweeter and less bitter** than a standard espresso — the bitter compounds extract later in the shot.
- Ristrettos excel as the base for **Flat Whites** — their concentrated sweetness pairs beautifully with microfoam.
- **Temperature:** 90–94°C — slightly lower end helps emphasise sweetness.
