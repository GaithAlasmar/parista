# London Fog

An elegant, café-style take on Earl Grey tea. **A strong Earl Grey brew is enriched with vanilla syrup and topped with silky steamed milk** — creating a lightly floral, creamy, and deeply comforting tea latte. Named after the foggy skies of London and the bergamot-grey of Earl Grey.

---

## Ingredients

- **1–2** Earl Grey tea bags (or 5g loose leaf Earl Grey)
- **80ml** hot water — **95°C** (just off boil — do not use 100°C boiling water for Earl Grey)
- **15ml** Monin Vanilla Syrup
- **160ml** whole milk, steamed to silky microfoam

---

## Instructions

1. **Brew the Earl Grey.** Steep **1–2 Earl Grey tea bags** in **80ml of 95°C hot water** for **3–4 minutes**. Remove the bags (do not squeeze — squeezing releases tannins that make the tea bitter).
2. **Add vanilla syrup.** Stir **15ml of Monin Vanilla Syrup** into the brewed tea concentrate. This sweetens the drink and balances the bergamot.
3. **Steam the milk.** Steam **180ml** of whole milk to **60–65°C** with smooth, velvety microfoam.
4. **Combine.** Pour the Earl Grey-vanilla concentrate into a pre-warmed glass or cup. Then slowly pour the steamed milk over the top.
5. **Optional latte art.** The milky surface provides a clean canvas for a simple heart or freehand pour.
6. Serve immediately with optional lavender or dried lemon garnish.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Do not over-steep Earl Grey** — 3–4 minutes maximum. Over-steeped Earl Grey becomes bitter and the bergamot turns harsh.
- **Water temperature at 95°C** (not boiling) — black tea is most aromatic brewed just below 100°C.
- For an **Iced London Fog:** brew concentrate → add syrup → let cool → ice → cold milk.
- **Lavender addition:** a drop of Monin Lavender Syrup (5ml) alongside the vanilla creates a "Lavender London Fog" — an elegant upgrade.
- The vanilla-bergamot combination is uniquely fragrant — this drink sells itself on aroma alone.
