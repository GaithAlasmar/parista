# Blue Ocean Mojito

A stunning, coffee-free built-in-the-cup refresher — **Monin Blue Curaçao Syrup** and **Monin Green Apple Syrup** over ice, topped with sparkling water for a fizzy, tropical, dazzling blue-green drink. **Do not blend** — the layers and the bubbles are the spectacle.

---

## Ingredients

- Ice cubes (fill the cup 2/3 full)
- 25 ml **Monin Blue Curaçao Syrup**
- 15 ml **Monin Green Apple Syrup**
- 150–180 ml sparkling water *(or still water for a non-fizzy version)*
- Fresh mint leaves — optional garnish
- Lime wedge — optional garnish

---

## Method

1. **Fill the cup with ice cubes** — large cubes work best for this build (they slow dilution and look cleaner).
2. **Add the syrups directly over the ice:**
   - Pour **25 ml Monin Blue Curaçao Syrup** first.
   - Pour **15 ml Monin Green Apple Syrup** second.
   - The two syrups will swirl as they hit the ice.
3. **Top with sparkling water.** Pour **150–180 ml sparkling water** slowly down the side of the cup — not directly onto the ice — to preserve the carbonation.
4. **Do not stir.** The colour gradient (deep blue at the base, lighter teal-green mid-layer) is the visual selling point of this drink.
5. **Garnish** with a sprig of fresh mint and/or a lime wedge on the rim.
6. Serve immediately with a wide straw so the customer can stir it themselves.

---

## Tips

- **Never blend this drink** — it is a built mojito-style refresher, not a slush. Blending destroys the colour layers and the carbonation.
- The **Green Apple Syrup** cuts through the sweetness of the Blue Curaçao and gives the drink a citrusy edge.
- For a **still water** version: same build, replace sparkling water — still delicious, just no fizz.
- This is one of the most photogenic drinks on the menu — position it near a window if presenting for social media.
