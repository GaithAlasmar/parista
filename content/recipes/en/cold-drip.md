# Cold Drip
A slow dripping cold water method producing a highly concentrated, smooth, and low-acidity brew, often served over ice.

---

## Ingredients
- 50g of medium-fine ground coffee
- 500ml of cold water (or cold water mixed with ice)
- 1:10 coffee to water ratio

---

## Instructions
1. Place a filter at the bottom of the coffee chamber of your cold drip tower and rinse it.
2. Add the 50g of ground coffee into the chamber. Tap gently to level it completely.
3. Place a secondary paper filter or a specialized dispersion screen on top of the coffee bed to ensure even water distribution.
4. Pre-wet the coffee gently with a small amount of water so the entire bed is damp.
5. Fill the top reservoir with 500ml of cold water and ice.
6. Open the valve and adjust the drip rate to approximately 1 drop per second.
7. Let the system run its course, which usually takes between 4 to 12 hours depending on the grind and drip rate.
8. Once finished, stir the extracted coffee concentrate to homogenize it.
9. Serve over ice, diluting with water or milk as desired.

---

## Pro-Tips
- Regularly check the drip rate during the first hour, as the water level drops the pressure decreases and the dripping might slow down.
- A paper filter on top of the coffee bed is essential to prevent channeling and ensure the water spreads evenly across the coffee.
