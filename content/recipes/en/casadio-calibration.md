# Casadio Machine & Grinder Calibration

Proper calibration of the **Casadio Nettuno** and grinder is the foundation of every great shot. This bar runs **Gia coffee beans** (black bags) as the primary espresso blend, with **Miscela Bar** as an alternative single-origin rotation. Even a small deviation in grind size, dose, or pressure shifts the flavour profile dramatically. Run this routine at the start of every shift and whenever you switch beans.

---

## 1. Target Parameters at a Glance

| Parameter | Target Range |
|---|---|
| Dose (dry grounds) | 18 – 20 g |
| Yield (liquid espresso) | 36 – 40 ml |
| Extraction time | 25 – 30 seconds |
| Boiler pressure | 1.0 – 1.2 bar |
| Pump pressure | 9 bar |
| Water temperature | 92 – 94 °C |
| Bean stock (primary) | Gia coffee beans |
| Bean stock (rotation) | Miscela Bar |

---

## 2. Machine Pressure Setup — Casadio Nettuno

### Boiler Pressure
- Allow the **Casadio Nettuno** to heat for a minimum of **20–25 minutes** before calibrating.
- Locate the **boiler pressure gauge** on the front panel — it should read **1.0 – 1.2 bar** at idle.
- If outside this range, adjust via the pressure stat screw or contact a Casadio-certified technician.
- **Never** adjust boiler pressure during service hours.

### Pump Pressure
- Trigger a flush with a **blind basket** (no grounds) in the group head.
- Read the **pump pressure gauge** during the flush — target is exactly **9 bar**.
- To adjust via the rotary pump adjustment screw:
  - **Clockwise** → increase pressure
  - **Counter-clockwise** → decrease pressure
- Re-test after each quarter-turn increment until you hold a stable 9 bar for at least 5 seconds.

---

## 3. Grinder Calibration

### Step 1 – Purge Old Grounds
- Grind and discard approximately **10–15 g** to flush the chamber of stale grounds.
- Critical after overnight rest **or** switching between Gia beans and Miscela Bar.

### Step 2 – Dose Check (Gia Beans)
- Grind directly into a **dry, pre-weighed portafilter basket**.
- Target: **18 – 20 g** (precision scale, accurate to 0.1 g).
- Note: **Gia beans** (medium-dark roast) typically produce a slightly more compacted grind — dial in one click finer vs. lighter roast blends when switching.
- **Miscela Bar** (medium roast) may require 0.5 – 1 click coarser than Gia — re-calibrate whenever rotating.

### Step 3 – Grind Size Adjustment
- Distribute and tamp with **~20 kg of even pressure**.
- Pull a test shot and time from when you press the extraction button:
  - **Under 25 seconds** → grind **finer** (toward "–")
  - **Over 30 seconds** → grind **coarser** (toward "+")
- One click at a time. After each adjustment, discard one shot before pulling the calibration shot.

### Step 4 – Yield Verification
- Tare a cup on the scale under the group head.
- Pull shot and measure liquid yield: target **36 – 40 ml**.
- Adjust dose or grind size if outside range.

---

## 4. Bean-Specific Notes

### Gia Beans (Primary Stock)
- Medium-dark roast — rich body, chocolate/nutty notes.
- Initial calibration setting: typically 1 click finer than the midpoint of your grinder's range.
- Degassing: Gia bags are valve-sealed. Optimal extraction after opening: **3–10 days** post-roast date.

### Miscela Bar (Rotation Stock)
- Balanced medium roast — mild acidity, smooth finish, designed for Italian-style espresso.
- Usually requires **0.5–1 click coarser** than Gia at the same dose.
- Document grinder click position in the calibration log whenever switching.

---

## 5. Calibration Log (Daily Record)

Log every shift calibration:

- Date & shift
- Bean used (Gia or Miscela Bar)
- Grinder click position
- Dose (g) / Yield (ml) / Time (seconds)
- Boiler pressure (bar) / Pump pressure (bar)
- Tasting notes

> **Tip:** If in-spec on time and yield but tasting bitter or sour, adjust water temperature by ±1 °C or reassess bean freshness — do not change grind first.

---

## 6. Common Calibration Issues

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Shot runs fast (< 25 s) & sour | Grind too coarse / under-dosed | Grind finer / increase dose |
| Shot runs slow (> 30 s) & bitter | Grind too fine / over-dosed | Grind coarser / reduce dose |
| Channelling | Uneven distribution or tamp | Level grounds; re-tamp evenly |
| Pressure inconsistent during pull | Pump fault or scale build-up | Descale group head; check pump |
| Low boiler pressure on Casadio | Heating element or stat fault | Contact Casadio technician |

---

## 7. End-of-Shift Procedure

- Backflush each group head on the **Casadio Nettuno** with a blind basket + group head cleaner (e.g., Puly Caff) for 5 cycles.
- Wipe group head gaskets and shower screens.
- Empty and rinse portafilter baskets.
- Record final boiler and pump pressure readings in the calibration log before shutdown.
- Seal open Gia or Miscela Bar bean bags airtight.
