# French Press

The most forgiving full-immersion brewer. **30g of coarsely ground coffee steeps in 500ml of hot water for 4 minutes**, then a metal plunger filters the grounds. The result is a rich, full-bodied cup with natural oils and a heavier mouthfeel than filter methods — bold and satisfying.

---

## Ingredients

- **30g** coarsely ground coffee — **Gia beans** or **Miscela Bar**
- **500ml** hot water — **93–96°C**
- French Press (1L or 8-cup capacity)

---

## Instructions

1. **Pre-warm.** Pour hot water into the French Press, swirl, and discard to pre-warm the vessel and minimise temperature loss.
2. **Add coffee.** Grind **30g** of coffee on a coarse setting (like raw sugar or breadcrumbs). Add to the French Press.
3. **Pour.** Pour **500ml** of 93–96°C water over the grounds. Ensure all grounds are saturated. Stir once gently.
4. **Place the lid.** Place the lid on (plunger up — do NOT press yet). Start the timer.
5. **Steep for 4 minutes.** Do not touch or stir during steeping.
6. **Press.** After exactly 4 minutes, press the plunger down **slowly and steadily** with even pressure. Do not press too fast (it will disturb the grounds and make the coffee gritty) or too slowly (over-extraction).
7. **Pour immediately.** Once pressed, pour all the coffee into a serving vessel or cups immediately. **Do NOT leave coffee in the French Press after pressing** — it will continue extracting and become bitter.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Coarse grind is essential:** Fine grind will slip through the metal filter, producing a muddy, over-extracted cup.
- **Pour immediately after pressing** — leaving it in the French Press is the most common mistake. Every minute after pressing adds bitterness.
- **French Press ratio:** 1:16.7 (30g coffee : 500ml water). For a stronger brew, use 35g.
- The French Press retains **coffee oils** that paper filters remove — this gives it a heavier, richer body. Some customers prefer this "natural" mouthfeel.
- A **4-minute steep is the sweet spot** — 3 minutes is under-extracted (sour); 5+ minutes is over-extracted (bitter).
