# Breve

The American luxury latte. A Breve replaces the standard whole milk with **half-and-half (50% milk, 50% cream)**, producing a dramatically richer, thicker, and more indulgent drink. Pronounced "BREH-vay."

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans**
- **120ml** half-and-half (equal mix of whole milk and cream)
- Light foam on top

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract **9g** of **Gia beans** into a pre-warmed cup.
2. **Prepare half-and-half.** Mix **60ml whole milk** and **60ml fresh cream** in the steaming pitcher.
3. **Steam the half-and-half.** Steam to **60–65°C**. The higher fat content means the foam will be extra thick and stable — control the steam wand carefully to avoid over-foaming.
4. **Pour.** Pour the steamed half-and-half into the espresso, leaving a light foam layer on top.
5. Serve immediately. The drink will be visibly thicker and creamier than a standard latte.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Steam carefully:** Half-and-half foams much more aggressively than whole milk due to higher fat content. Use shorter bursts of air introduction.
- The rich fat content **softens the espresso bitterness** significantly — a good suggestion for customers who find lattes too sharp.
- **Temperature:** Keep to 60–65°C. Over-heating cream causes a greasy, unpleasant texture.
- For a cold version, use cold half-and-half directly over ice with espresso — an Iced Breve.
