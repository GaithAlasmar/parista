# Brown Sugar Iced Shaken Espresso

A trendy, highly requested iced drink that relies on vigorous shaking to chill the espresso instantly and create a thick, stable foam. Sweetened with brown sugar and typically topped with oat milk.

---

## Ingredients

- **30 ml** double espresso — **Gia beans**
- **15 ml** Monin Brown Sugar Syrup (or 2 bar spoons of raw brown sugar)
- **Dash** of cinnamon powder
- **200 g** ice (plus extra for serving)
- **60 ml** oat milk (or whole milk)

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso:** Extract a double shot of Gia espresso.
2. **Prepare the shaker:** In a cocktail shaker, add 15 ml of Monin Brown Sugar Syrup, a dash of cinnamon, and the freshly pulled hot espresso. Swirl to dissolve the sugar/syrup.
3. **Add ice:** Fill the shaker halfway with ice.
4. **Shake vigorously:** Cap the shaker and shake hard for 10-15 seconds until the shaker is frosty outside and a thick foam forms inside.
5. **Serve:** Pour the entire contents (including ice and foam) into a serving glass. If needed, add a few more fresh ice cubes.
6. **Top with milk:** Gently pour 60 ml of oat milk over the top. It will cascade beautifully through the espresso.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Vigorous shaking:** The key to this drink is the shaking process. It must be shaken hard enough to aerate the espresso, producing a rich, creamy foam that mimics dairy.
- **Do not shake the milk:** Only shake the espresso, syrup, and ice. The milk is strictly a splash on top.
- **Cinnamon integration:** Adding the cinnamon to the shaker rather than sprinkling it on top allows the flavour to infuse evenly into the coffee.
