# AeroPress

The most versatile manual brewing device in specialty coffee. The **AeroPress uses air pressure to push water through coffee in under 2 minutes**, producing a concentrated, smooth, and low-acid cup. Virtually no rules — the AeroPress rewards experimentation.

---

## Ingredients

- **17g** medium-fine ground coffee — **Gia beans** or any origin
- **250ml** hot water — **88–96°C** (temperature affects flavour dramatically)
- AeroPress brewer + paper or metal filter
- Scale and timer

---

## Instructions (Standard Method)

1. **Set up.** Place a paper filter in the AeroPress cap and rinse it with hot water. Attach the cap to the chamber. Place the AeroPress on a stable mug or server.
2. **Add coffee.** Grind **17g** to a medium-fine consistency. Add to the chamber.
3. **Bloom.** Pour **40ml** of water at 88–96°C over the grounds. Stir gently for 10 seconds to fully saturate. Wait **20–30 seconds**.
4. **Full pour.** Pour remaining water to reach **250ml** total. Stir once.
5. **Insert plunger.** Gently insert the plunger and press down with **steady, controlled pressure** for ~20–30 seconds. Stop when you hear a hiss of air.
6. Serve the concentrate as-is, or dilute with 50–100ml hot water for an Americano-style cup.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Inverted method:** Many baristas brew AeroPress inverted (plunger side down) for a longer, more controlled steep — then flip and press. This allows more brew time before pressing.
- **Temperature flexibility:** 88°C for lighter, fruitier notes; 92–96°C for fuller, darker extraction.
- **Pressure matters:** Slow, steady pressure = fuller body. Fast pressure = thinner, brighter cup.
- AeroPress brews in **under 2 minutes** and is nearly impossible to make truly bad — ideal for training baristas on extraction concepts.
- The AeroPress is one of the **few brewing methods where experimentation is always encouraged** — adjust grind, temperature, dose, and steep time freely.
