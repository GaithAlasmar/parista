# Cappuccino

The iconic Italian coffee in perfect thirds: **espresso, steamed milk, and foam** in equal measure. A cappuccino is defined by its thick, dry foam layer — the contrast between the bold espresso below and the cloud-like foam above is the point.

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans**
- **60ml** steamed whole milk
- **60ml** dry foam (thick, airy)

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract **9g** of **Gia beans** into a pre-warmed cappuccino cup (150–180ml ceramic).
2. **Steam the milk.** Steam **180–200ml** of whole milk to **60–65°C**. For a cappuccino, create **more foam** than for a latte — tilt the pitcher and introduce more air for the first 3–4 seconds of steaming.
3. **Spoon out the foam.** Use a spoon to hold back the foam as you pour the steamed milk into the espresso (~60ml liquid milk). Then spoon the thick foam on top (~60ml).
4. The foam should be **stiff enough** to hold a small amount of cocoa powder or cinnamon sprinkled on top.
5. Serve with a dusting of cocoa powder if desired.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Foam consistency for a cappuccino:** target stiff, pillowy foam that holds its shape — not wet microfoam (that's for lattes).
- **Classic ratio:** 1/3 espresso : 1/3 milk : 1/3 foam. Deviating too far turns it into a latte.
- For a **dry cappuccino:** all foam, almost no steamed milk. For a **wet cappuccino:** more steamed milk, lighter foam (closer to a latte).
- **Children's cappuccino:** use only **50–55°C** milk temperature — same ratio but cooler.
