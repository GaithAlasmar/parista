# Kyoto Coffee
An elegant Japanese slow-drip cold brew method, visually striking and capable of producing a complex, wine-like cup with intense flavor clarity.

---

## Ingredients
- 60g of medium ground coffee
- 600ml of filtered cold water (often ice water)
- 1:10 coffee to water ratio

---

## Instructions
1. Prepare the middle beaker by placing a ceramic or cloth filter at the bottom.
2. Add 60g of medium ground coffee. Level the bed meticulously without compressing it.
3. Place a round paper filter exactly the size of the beaker on top of the grounds to disperse water droplets evenly.
4. Pre-saturate the grounds with about 50ml of water to ensure an even extraction from the start.
5. Fill the top glass globe with the remaining cold water and ice cubes.
6. Adjust the brass or glass valve to a slow, steady drip rate of 1 drop every 1-2 seconds.
7. Allow the brewing process to complete undisturbed over the next 8 to 24 hours.
8. Swirl the final carafe to mix the denser first drips with the lighter final drips.
9. Serve in a chilled glass, neat or over large clear ice cubes.

---

## Pro-Tips
- The visual appeal of Kyoto towers is matched by their sensitivity; avoid placing the tower in direct sunlight or warm areas during brewing.
- Experiment with light roasts (especially naturals) to unlock unique, fruit-forward flavor notes rarely found in standard cold brew.
