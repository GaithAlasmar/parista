# Macchiato

Italian for "stained" or "marked." A shot of espresso with just a **small dot of steamed milk and foam** on top — enough to soften the edge of the espresso without fundamentally changing it. Not to be confused with the sweetened caramel macchiato.

---

## Ingredients

- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans**
- **~20ml** steamed whole milk with a small amount of foam

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract a single shot (9g) into a small espresso cup.
2. **Steam a small amount of milk.** Steam just enough milk (~50ml in the pitcher) to create a small amount of microfoam. Target **60–65°C**.
3. **Mark the espresso.** Pour a small spoonful (~20ml) of steamed milk with foam onto the centre of the espresso shot. The milk should "stain" the crema.
4. Serve immediately. The macchiato should show a white dot in a golden-brown crema.

---

## Pro-Tips

- The key distinction: a macchiato is **espresso with a touch of milk** — not a milk drink with espresso.
- Use whole milk for a creamier texture; the small volume means fat content has a significant impact on taste.
- **Milk temperature:** 60–65°C — even for such a small amount, cold milk will cool the espresso too quickly.
- For a **double macchiato**: pull a doppio (18g) and mark with the same small amount of milk.
