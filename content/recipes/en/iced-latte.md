# Iced Latte

The cold version of the classic latte — **double espresso poured over cold whole milk and ice**. Clean, simple, and endlessly popular. The foundation for all iced flavoured lattes.

---

## Ingredients

- **250ml** ice
- **180ml** cold whole milk
- **30ml** single espresso (9g) — **Gia beans**

---

## Instructions

1. **Pull the espresso.** Extract **9g** of **Gia beans** into a shot glass. Set aside — no need to cool first.
2. **Fill the cup with ice.** Pack a large cup (~400ml) with ice cubes.
3. **Add cold milk.** Pour **180ml cold whole milk** over the ice.
4. **Pour the espresso.** Add the espresso shot directly over the milk. It will create a beautiful layered gradient — lighter milk below, dark espresso floating briefly on top.
5. Serve immediately with a straw. Stir before drinking to blend.

---

## Pro-Tips

- For a **stronger iced latte**, use a double espresso (18g / 60ml) as the base — this is the specialty café standard.
- **Do not use hot brewed espresso that has been cooled** — always pull a fresh shot and pour it hot over the cold milk. The temperature contrast is part of the experience.
- For a **flavoured iced latte**, add **15–20ml of Monin syrup** to the bottom of the cup before the ice.
- **Whole milk** gives the best mouthfeel — oat milk is an excellent alternative for a dairy-free option.
