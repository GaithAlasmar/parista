# Lungo

Italian for "long." More water pushed through the same espresso dose, producing a larger, more diluted, and slightly more bitter shot. The lungo is for customers who want an espresso-style drink but find the standard shot too intense.

---

## Ingredients

- **9g** (single) or **18g** (double) freshly ground coffee — **Gia beans** or **Miscela Bar**
- Hot water pushed through to **60ml yield**

---

## Instructions

1. **Dose.** Grind **9g** of coffee for a single lungo, or **18g** for a double lungo.
2. **Distribute and tamp** as standard.
3. **Lock and pull.** Start extraction and let it run longer than a standard espresso.
4. **Stop at ~60ml** for a single, or until the extraction begins to taste bitter/hollow.
5. The shot will have a lighter, thinner crema compared to a ristretto or standard espresso — this is expected.
6. Serve immediately in a small glass or espresso cup.

---

## Pro-Tips

- A lungo is **not an Americano** — no hot water is added after the fact. The lungo pulls extra water through the grounds during extraction.
- The extended pull extracts more **bitter compounds** — use this selectively; Miscela Bar beans are more forgiving for lungos than Gia.
- **Water temperature:** 90–96°C standard.
- If a customer asks for a "weaker espresso," suggest a lungo or an Americano — they are distinct experiences.
