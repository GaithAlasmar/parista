# Chemex

The most visually iconic manual brewer — the **Chemex produces an exceptionally clean, bright, and tea-like cup** due to its thick, bonded paper filter that removes almost all oils and fine particles. A 30g coffee to 500ml water brew that is as beautiful to watch as it is to drink.

---

## Ingredients

- **30g** coarsely ground coffee — single origin or **Gia beans**
- **500ml** hot water — **92–96°C**
- Chemex brewer (6-cup or 8-cup)
- Chemex bonded paper filter (square or pre-folded)
- Gooseneck kettle
- Scale and timer

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the filter.** Open the Chemex paper filter with 3 layers facing the spout side. Place in the Chemex. Rinse thoroughly with hot water — this seals the filter to the glass and removes any papery taste. Discard rinse water.
2. **Add the coffee.** Grind **30g** of coffee to a medium-coarse setting (slightly coarser than V60). Add to the filter and level gently.
3. **Bloom.** Start the timer. Pour **60ml** of 92–96°C water in circles over the grounds. Wait **45 seconds** for the bloom — Chemex brews are large and benefit from a longer bloom.
4. **First pour.** At 45 seconds, begin pouring slowly in concentric spirals. Pour to **200ml** total by ~1:30.
5. **Second pour.** Continue to **350ml** total by ~2:30.
6. **Final pour.** Complete to **500ml** total by ~3:30.
7. **Drain.** Allow to drain fully. Total brew time: **4:30–5:00 minutes**.
8. Remove the filter and serve directly from the Chemex vessel.

---

## Pro-Tips

- **Grind slightly coarser than V60** — the Chemex's thick filter slows flow, and a coarser grind compensates.
- The Chemex's thick bonded filter removes **most oils and fine sediment** — the result is an exceptionally clear, clean cup. This is by design.
- **Chemex ratio:** 1:16.7 (30g coffee : 500ml water). Adjust to taste: 1:15 for stronger, 1:18 for lighter.
- The Chemex is ideal for showcasing **light-roast single origins** — its clarity reveals delicate fruity and floral notes that would be masked in an espresso.
- Serve in the Chemex vessel at the table for a premium, ceremonial presentation.
